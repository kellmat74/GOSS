# COMMAND & GENS PROCEDURES
Source: GOSS Player Aids v3 2020, Pages 13–14 (PAC 11, v4)

---

## JOINT COMMAND PHASE (3.3.1c)

### Command Segment:

Both sides may:  
**1)** Attach and/or detach units/formations (9.6.0).  
**Note:** Emergency Command Changes (9.4.1).  
**2)** Change corps and army boundaries (9.2.0).  
**3)** Enter/release formations to/from refit (22.4.0).  
**4)** Enter/release formations to/from army reserve (25.0).

**Army (9.3.1):** Any number of corps may be assigned to an army. A max of 12 asset units may be assigned to an army HQ. Any Div and BG in Strat mode or army reserve (5.5.0 & 25.0) may be assigned to an army HQ.  
**Corps (9.3.2):** Any number of formations may be assigned to a corps. A max of 24 asset units may be assigned to a corps HQ.

## FORMATIONS (9.3.3)

a) A Div formation consists of assigned and/or attached Bdes, Rgts, CCs and KG and a variable number of asset units such as AT, Flak, Eng, Recon and Art.  
b) Bdes, CCs and KGs not assigned or attached to a Div HQ and instead operating independently, are BG formations.

**Detachment Limit (9.6.1):** No more than 6 assigned units may detach from a formation.  
**Attachment Limit (9.6.2):** Formations may attach a max of:  
a) No more than 6 units.  
b) No more than 4 of the 6 may be larger than a 1 step Co.  
c) No more than 1 may be an Art unit.  
**Special Assignments (9.6.2a):** Players may assign units to a formation to replace eliminated assigned units (9.6.2a).  
**US Tank, TD & AT (9.6.2b):** Each US Div may attach 1 Tk & either 1 TD or 1 towed AT Bn. They do not count against the division's max attachment (9.6.2) or fuel requirements (16.4.4).  
**German RE Units:** May be attached to German formations (9.6.2c). RE units may be assigned using 9.6.2a.  
**US Armored Cavalry Groups (9.6.2d):** No more than two Arm Recon Bn.'s can be attached to a single BG.  
**CW Arm Bdes (9.6.2e):** Additional units cannot be attached to a CW Arm Bde.  
**German KG (9.6.2f):** A named KG (example: KG Peiper) with more than 6 units can form a BG. A KG with 6 or more units may not attach additional units.

## ADMINISTRATIVE PHASE (3.3.2g)

### Air Resupply Segment: (AM & PM GTs only):

Active player conducts air supply missions (20.5.0).  
- AM or PM GTs only.  
- Clear or POvr atmospheric condition only.  
1) Declare the number of ATP conducting the mission.  
2) Declare the number of ASup AP escorting the mission.  
3) Declare the number of AP that will intercept the mission.  
4) Conduct ASup (if any).  
5) Declare the location of the drop zone hex (DZ).  
6) Resolve the drop.

The DZ must be clear, hedgerow or rough terrain. May contain location or village. Not enemy occupied, or adjacent to enemy unit.  
Roll 1d10 for each ATP. Apply the following DRM:  
**+1:** For every 4 enemy units within 3 hexes of DZ. A in range Flak capable unit (20.7.1) counts as 2 units.  
**+1:** If the atmospheric condition is POvr.  
If modified DR is 8 or less, each surviving ATP delivers 1 air supply Pt.  
Unmodified DR of zero always succeeds.

### Adding OhS (15.5.1b): Each Pt of air supply delivers:

a) 1 OhS Pt to an army HQ.  
b) 2 OhS Pt to a corps HQ.  
c) 4 OhS Pts to a Div or BG HQ.  
- HQ must be able to trace GenS path no longer than 3 hexes to DZ.  
- If there is no HQ to receive the supply, 1 air supply Pt will allow: Player to remove 10 OoS markers from any units (except HQs) within 3 hexes of the DZ.

### Supply Determination Segment (AM, PM, & Night GTs):

**1)** The active player determines the supply status of all HQs (15.1.1).  
**HQ to HQ Supply Path (15.2.2):** GenS paths are traced from a PSS to an HQ (could be from PSS to depot to an HQ) or from an HQ to a subordinate HQ.  
They can only trace through:  
a) Clear terrain (when GC is normal/dry only); **or,**  
b) Secondary or primary roads (not trails).  
- HQs must use Mech class GenS path procedures (15.2.1a).  
- HQ paths cannot exceed 18 Mech class MP unless using an extended GenS path (15.2.4).  
- If a player cannot trace a valid GenS path to an HQ, that HQ goes into an OhS status.  
- An HQ in OhS (15.5.0) can only trace a GenS path to its subordinate HQ/units that is ½ its normal GenS path distance.  

**2)** Active player checks GenS state of all non-HQ units (15.2.3).  
**Leg Formations:** 12 Leg class MP.  
**Axis Mech Formations:** 12 Mech class MP.  
**Allied Mech Formations:** 18 Mech class MP.  
- All types of HQ may use extended GenS paths (15.2.4).  
- Mech formation HQs may only trace GenS paths to Mech class units using a Mech GenS path.  
- Mech formation HQ may trace to Leg units using Mech or Leg GenS paths. Decision made on a unit by unit basis.  
a) Make this decision prior to tracing a GenS path to each individual Leg unit.  
b) Each unit's GenS path may only utilize 1 movement class rate during the current phase.  

When an Allied Mech HQ traces GenS path using Leg class movement, reduce the GenS path to 12 Leg class MP.  
- If a player assigns 1 TP from the army TP to a Leg HQ, all units of formation can use Mech class GenS movement. Does not cancel extended GenS penalties (15.2.4a).  
- If a unit is in GenS, then all adjacent friendly units subordinate to the same HQ are in GenS if the hexside between the 2 units does not prohibit Leg movement or is un-bridged river hexside.  
a) Units in GenS this way may not provide GenS to other units.  
b) This does not allow units to move into an OoS hex (7.7.5).

**Important:** Eng units may allow tracing a Leg GenS path by ferrying GenS over a river/stream (15.2.1b).

## SUPPLY PATHS & WEATHER (15.2.6)

**Deep Mud:** Exclusively on Primary Road; ½ MP & on Secondary Road 1 MP.  
**Deep Mud:** Not exclusively on primary/secondary road is ½ the normal GenS length. Deep mud only applies if listed in scenario rules.  
**Mud:** Not exclusively on primary/secondary roads; -3 MP.  
**Snow:** Not exclusively on primary/secondary roads; -1 MP.

## ON HAND SUPPLY (15.5.0)

A unit with an OhS marker continues in that supply status until it has expended all OhS, or the unit has a valid GenS path during a friendly Supply Determination Segment (15.1.2).  
- An OhS path is ½ the normal GenS path (15.2.5).  
- If a unit conducts activity that would use its on-hand supply, mark the unit with an OoS marker.  
- HQs using OhS may operate normally and provide GenS to subordinate units until the HQ is OoS.

**HQs & On Hand Supply (15.5.1):** Roll 1d10 and apply the following cumulative DRMs:  
**-4:** If a formation HQ.  
**-3:** If corps HQ.  
**-1:** If unable to trace a GenS path of any length to the HQ.  
**+1:** If the HQ is in a town or city hex.  
**+2:** If it is a US HQ.  
- If the modified DR is:  
**3 or less:** The on-hand supply number is 4.  
**4–6:** The on-hand supply number is 6.  
**7 or more:** The on-hand supply number is 8.

**Expending HQ OhS (15.5.1a):** During the Command Phase convert OhS points to fuel points (FP) or AmP and expend OhS points to supply subordinate HQ and units.  
- Each HQ OhS point expended allows an HQ to be a supply source for all its subordinate HQs and assigned units until the next Command Phase. OhS HQs that start the Command Phase with no OhS points immediately become OoS.  
- A player may convert OhS points to fuel points. One OhS point converted creates one fuel point.  
- An OhS HQ may convert OhS points to AmP. The size of the HQ affects the number of AmP received for each point converted. The player may use these AmP to replenish AD Art units subordinate to the HQ.  
a) 1 army OhS point yields 2 AmP.  
b) 1 corps OhS point yields 1 AmP.  
- OhS HQs have an ADV equal to the remaining OhS points after deducting points to provide GenS and converting OhS points to FP and/or AmP. All subordinate Art units use this ADV when checking for AD and replenishment.

**Units & OhS Usage (15.5.2):** All non-HQ units that cannot trace an OhS supply path to an HQ with available OhS points or an HQ in that is in GenS are marked OhS. Non-HQ units marked with an OhS remain in OhS until the unit conducts one of the following actions:  
1) Expend more than ½ of their printed MA; **or,**  
2) Conduct an offensive GA; **or,**  
3) Defend against a GA using more than ½ their defensive CS; **or,**  
4) Participate in an Art FS mission using more than ½ of its BF (11.4.1); **or,**  
5) Expend more than 3 MPs while in Exploit mode. A unit in Exploit mode may conduct an overrun and not become OoS if it uses no more than 3 MPs and participates in the GA using only ½ of its attack CS.  
- Immediately mark any unit that performed the above actions with an OoS marker.

### Ammo Replenishment Segment:

**Step 1:** The active side may replenish AD Art units by expending AmP (16.3.7a).  
**Step 2:** The active side rolls 1d10 to replenish AD Art units (16.3.7b).  
**Step 3:** Inactive side may replenish AD Art units by expending AmP (16.3.7a).

## OOS EFFECTS (15.6.0)

- Units that are OoS cannot:  
1) Enter PA mode.  
2) Enter Exploit mode.  
3) Enter Strat mode.  
4) Enter CR or provide CR bonuses.  
5) Conduct replacement.  
6) Conduct any type of construction or demolition (other than IPs).

**Exception:** OoS Eng units conduct construction as non-Eng units.

- If units are OoS:  
1) They may only expend ½ of their printed MA (round up).  
2) They must immediately exit Exploit mode.  
3) Eng units lose all combat Eng abilities.  
4) Reduce their CS (attack and defense) by ½ (round up).  
5) Art units are marked AD and may not replenish.  
6) If isolated, they may be subject to surrender (15.7.1).

**Important:** Do not modify the Arm/AT factors of units that are OoS.

- OoS HQ units:  
1) May not provide GenS to subordinate units.  
2) Cannot count any of its subordinates as the nearest air observer for Allied air observation (8.3.0).

**Isolation (15.7.0):** A unit that cannot trace a GenS path of any distance is isolated if there are no friendly units in GenS within three (two intervening) hexes of the unit. Note it is possible to be isolated and OoS or isolated and OhS (15.1.2).  
- Players may not designate an isolated unit as a spotter for FS missions.  
- During the Command Phase, each side checks to see if any isolated OoS units surrender (15.7.1).  
- Isolated OoS units must check for surrender prior to defending in GA (13.5.1).

## SURRENDER (15.7.1)

During the Joint Command Phase both players determine if isolated units surrender by conducting a defensive PRC for each isolated/OoS unit. Modify the DR:  
**+2:** For each AM (after the first) that a unit checks for surrender.  
**Important:** This DRM also applies if checking for surrender due to GA.  
**+1:** If any other unit within two hexes (one intervening hex) has already surrendered during that GT.  
**-1:** If stacked with a Ldr (23.2.1e).  
- If the unit fails the PRC, the unit surrenders; remove it from the map.

**Important:** Units that surrender or are eliminated in any manner while isolated do not garner recycle steps (22.2.0). Players must keep these units separate from other eliminated units as these units cost additional ReP when resurrecting them (22.5.2a).
