## MOVEMENT ORDER (7.5.2)

Move units individually. Special stacking (6.2.0) allows units to move as 1 unit. Units must be moved in the below order:  
**1)** Units in Strat mode.  
**2)** Units in Tac mode (5.1.0).  
**2a)** Mech class units using Mech road movement (7.10.0).  
**2b)** All units using non-road movement (7.11.0).  
**3)** Units in PA mode (5.2.3).

## MA MODIFICATIONS (7.6.0)

**Reductions to a Unit's MA (7.6.1):** When dividing a unit's MA always round up. Determine reductions in the order given in 7.6.1a through 7.6.1f.  
**Fuel (7.6.1a):** See 16.4.5 to determine a Mech unit's available MA.

**Increase Unit's MA (7.6.1b):** After applying Mods for fuel (7.6.1a) & before applying 7.6.1c & 7.6.1d, increase unit's MA (round up):  
a) Is in Strat mode (5.5.0).  
b) Is in a MR bonus period (5.4.4).  
c) Is a Leg class unit meeting all the requirement in 7.6.3.  
d) Is subject to a Ldr's bonus (23.2.1a). Ldr bonus is cumulative.  
**MA Halved (7.6.1c):** Halve unit's MA if the unit is: OoS, Fatigue 1 or is in Exploit mode in an AM or PM GT Exploit Phase.  
**MA Quartered (7.6.1d):** Quarter a unit's MA if: Any 2 or more halving conditions are in affect or the unit is fatigue 2. Never reduce unit's MA below ¼ of its MA due to conditions in 7.3.1c & 7.3.1d.  
**GC (7.6.1e):** After applying Mods in 7.6.1a/7.6.1d modify MA due to GC.  
**Wet:** Mech class units subtract 2 from modified MA unless using Mech road Move only along primary or secondary roads. No effect to Leg class.  
**Snow:**  
a) Mech class units divide modified MA by 3 if not using Mech road. Move only along primary or secondary roads.  
b) Mech class units subtract 1 from modified MA if using Mech road. Move only along primary or secondary roads.  
c) Leg units divide modified MA by 3 if not moving along primary or secondary roads only.  
**Mud:**  
a) Mech class units divide modified MA by 2 if not using Mech road movement only along primary/secondary roads.  
b) Leg class units divide modified MA by 2 if not moving only along primary/secondary roads.  
**Movement during Night GTs (7.6.1f):** Night GTs effect MP costs:  
- Mech class units not using Mech road Move entirely along primary/secondary roads expend 1 additional MP for each hex entered.  
- Leg class units expend 1 additional MP per hex, unless moving entirely along any type of road (including trails & RRs).  

**Leg Class Units & Roads (7.6.2):** Increase a Leg class unit's MA by 2 if the unit:  
a) Is in Tac mode.  
b) Is in GenS & in command.  
c) Starts on & moves entirely along primary or secondary roads.  
d) Does not start, move or end its move adjacent to an enemy unit.  
e) Is not observed by enemy ground unit at any time in Movement Phase.

## MOVEMENT CLASS CHANGES (7.12.0)

Units mount/dismount and/or breakdown/combine (18.0) during a friendly Movement Phase at a cost of ½ their MA.  
**Army TP (7.12.3a):** 1 TP transports 3 units (any size).  
**Allied Inherent Transport (7.12.3b):** Transports 3 units per Div HQ (any size).

## SPECIAL MOVEMENT RULES (7.12.4)

**Light AT:** MA of 6 to 9, move as Leg, or as MU with 6 MA.  
**Allied AT:** May move up to 1 hex as leg at start or end MA cost 6.  
**Allied Eng:** Have inherent TP, may mount/dismount per 7.8.0.  
**Cdo:** DR 0–4 ignore MH, no cost to move adj.  
**Artillery 1 hex:** 75, 105H, 155, 150, (not G) may move 1 hex in-battery.

## DELAY MARKERS (7.13.0)

To deploy a delay marker a unit must:  
a) Be a personnel type unit (Leg or Mech).  
b) Have a Def PR of 5 or greater.  
c) Have at least 2 steps remaining.  
d) Begin the Movement Phase in a hex with allowed terrain.  
e) Be in GenS & in command.  
f) Be in Tac mode.  
- Attempting unit expends ½ available MA (round up) & pass a Def PRC.  
**Pass:** Randomly pick a marker. Place with DRM down in the hex.  
**Fail:** Do not place marker.  
- Units that create a delay marker must vacate the hex. Movement must be toward the unit's source of GenS.  
- A delay marker generates a MH on enemy units entering hex. Units friendly to marker may move through the hex. Remove the marker if friendly unit ends any phase in the hex.  
- At the beginning of the Attack Designation Segment the active player determines the effect of delay markers stacked with friendly attack designated units.  
a) The player must conduct an Off PRC using the unit with lowest PR in the hex. Apply the DRM on the delay marker.  
**Pass:** Remove marker, no effect.  
**Fail:** Remove attack designation marker. Do not remove delay marker.  
b) During the Delay Marker Removal Segment, the owning player may remove any delay markers. The non-owning player may remove delay markers if a friendly unit occupies the hex.
