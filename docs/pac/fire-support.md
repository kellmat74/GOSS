# FIRE SUPPORT MISSION PROCEDURES
Source: GOSS Player Aids v3 2020, Pages 9–10 (PAC 9, v1)

## FS MISSION SEQUENCE (11.1.0)

**Sub-Segment 1:** Active side conducts GS.  
**Sub-Segment 2:** Inactive side (1) declares NGS/GS, (2) conducts Art, (3) conducts NGS or GS.  
**Sub-Segment 3:** Active side (1) declares NGS, (2) conducts Art, (3) conducts NGS.

### Resolving the Mission:

**Step 1:** Active player selects the spotting unit.  
**Step 2:** Inactive player informs active player if AFV are in any hex the spotting unit can observe.  
**Step 3:** Active player selects target hex.  
**Step 4:** Active player calculates the number of volleys (including intensive fire volleys)  
**Step 5:** Both players determine DRMs.  
**Step 6:** Determine the result.  
**Step 7:** Inactive player implements results.  
**Step 8:** Active player checks for AD.

## UNITS ALWAYS OBSERVED (11.2.3)

1) Units within LOS of an enemy ground unit or air observation; and, in open terrain, or, in Strat mode.  
2) Units adjacent to an enemy unit and,  
a) The enemy unit is attack designated; **or,**  
b) The friendly unit is attack designated; **or,**  
c) A unit is constructing a bridge (17.3.1); **or,**  
d) A unit is constructing an ET-2 & is not in MCT (17.3.3b).  
3) All units in 2) are observed by units in VP/town/city if observer can trace a LOS to the hex.  
4) All units Adj to Metz Fort.

## SPOTTING UNIT (11.2.2)

Spotter must be able to observe a unit, or population feature. If against observed population feature & no observed units, conduct as observed for mission capacity & apply unobserved DRM.  
**Spotter Limits (11.2.1a):** Bn. (any number of steps) may spot for 2 target hexes. 2-step hybrid is Bn. All Co. units may spot for missions into 1 hex.  
- Z-step units may not spot.  
- Max of 2 units may spot from a single hex.  
- Units advanced after a FS mission may not spot.

## MISSION CAPACITY (11.2.4)

**Light (11.2.4a):** Unobserved must be light.  
a) A Co. unit; **or,**  
b) A corps/army asset not assigned to a formation; **or,**  
c) A Bn. Being overrun & is not in ET/Fort/Ft Area.

### Light FS Mission Capacity

| Allied: 2 | Axis: 1 |
|---|---|
| GS Mission: No GS | NG Mission: 1 DD |

**Important:** To conduct Med/Hvy missions, target unit or population feature must be observed.

**Medium (11.2.4b):** Air observed must be medium FS mission.  
a) A Bn; **or,**  
b) A Co. in ET/Fort/Ft Area hex; **or,**  
c) A Co. or Bn. in ET/Fort/Ft Area being overrun. Attacker may not conduct NGS/GS during overrun. Spotter must be in overrun stack.

### Medium FS Mission Capacity

| Allied: 3 | Axis: 2 |
|---|---|
| GS Mission: 1 mission | NG Mission: 2 DD |

**Heavy (11.2.4c):**  
a) A Bn. in PA mode; **or,**  
b) A Bn. in an ET/Fort/Ft Area hex.

### Heavy FS Mission Capacity

| US: 16 | Other Allied: 12 | Axis: 8 |
|---|---|---|
| GS Mission: 2 missions | NG Mission: 1 BB or MN; **or,** 1 CA and 1 CL; **or,** 1 CA and 1 CL; **or,** 1 CL and 1 DD; **or,** 2 DD. |

**Modifications (11.2.4d):** +1 Art unit if spotter in VP. Do not count the below against capacity.  
a) Art units adjacent to the target hex.  
b) Hvy Flak or CW/US TD units.  
c) Forts with Art capabilities.

**Formation Requirements (11.2.5):** To participate in a Med/Hvy mission Art units must be:  
a) Subordinate to same formation as spotter. At least 1 Art must be subordinate to spotting unit's formation.  
b) Assigned to same army or corps as the spotter's formation.  
c) 1 other formation, assigned to same corps may contribute all assigned Art. See 15.4.3 for the effect if a formation/corps/army HQ is not supported.

**88mm Flak Co.:** Can only fire if 1 non-Flak unit fires. Flak Co.'s have range of 4, they do not check for AD, and are exempt from formation requirements (11.4.1b).  
**Allied TD:** (beginning Sept 1944) US/CW TD units may conduct FS missions (same as 88mm Flak). They have range of 2, must adhere to formation requirements, and cannot fire if Adj to enemy unit (11.4.1c).

## RESOLVE NUMERICAL HITS (11.6.2)

See 11.2.1 when resolving mission in hex containing attack designated & non-attack designated units.  
Choose 1 of the below 2 options:  
**1)** 1st hit as a step loss (11.6.2a); **or,**  
**2)** 1st hit as a retreat (11.6.2b).  
See scenario rules for Forts & units in Forts.  
See 4.1.3 to determine Z-step losses.  
- HQ cannot take losses until all non-HQ take 1 loss.  
- Unit cannot suffer additional losses until all units have taken an equal number of losses.

## 1ST NUMERICAL HIT AS STEP LOSS (11.6.2a)

Must use this if units cannot retreat 1 hex (11.6.3).  
**Exception:** If units are attack designated player may take 1st hit as retreat by withdrawing into DW in hex, even if units could not retreat 1 hex (11.6.2b).  
**Step 1:** Owning player takes one step loss from 1 unit; **or,**  
a) If non-attack designated units in ET-3, player may remove ET-3 to absorb loss. If hits remain, go to step 2. ET-3 may not negate PRC in step 2.  
b) If non-attack designated units in Fort, player may reduce Fort by 1 step to absorb 1 hit. If hits remain go to step 2. If Fort eliminated, Fort may not negate PRC in step 2.  
**Step 2, PRC:** If hits remain after step 1, conduct PRC use Def PR of lowest PR unit. Non-attack designated units in ET/Fort/Ft Area auto pass.  
**PRC Passed:** Units remain in hex. Attack designated units retain attack designation.  
**Important:** ET-3 do not absorb losses in this step.  
1) Apply 1 hit as a step loss.  
2) If hits remain:  
a) Convert 1 or 2 hits to fatigue hits; and/or,  
b) Apply all remaining hits as step losses (4.2.1). If units are in Fort treat Fort as unit.   

**PRC Failed:** Remove attack designation, apply 1 AS to all units (11.6.1); **and,**  
1) Retreat all units 1 hex or if units were attack designated, withdraw units into DW (Not IP). Withdrawal = 1 hex retreat. Do not reduce total hits.  
2) Apply hits as step losses (Apply max step loss). If units are not attack designated & in Fort, treat Fort as unit.  
3) If after reaching max step loss there are still hits, player may convert max of 2 hits to fatigue hits.  
4) After applying 2 fatigue hits, if hits remain, max step loss does not apply. Take remaining hits as step losses.

## 1ST NUMERICAL HIT AS RETREAT (11.6.2b)

Only use this option if units can retreat 1 hex (11.6.3).  
**Exception:** If units are attack designated & cannot retreat 1 hex, player may take 1st hit as retreat by withdrawing into a DW in the hex.  
**Step 1:** Remove attack designation from units.  
**Step 2:** All units suffer 1 AS hit (11.6.1).  
**Step 3:** Retreat units 1 hex (11.6.3) or if units were attack designated, may withdraw units into DW (Not IP). Withdrawal into DW fulfills retreat.  
**Step 4:** Subtract 1 hit from the total hits.  
**Step 5:** Divide remaining number by 2 (round up).  
a) The result is number of hits applied. Max step loss applies.  
b) If units reach max step loss, convert up to 2 hits to fatigue hits.  
c) After applying 2 fatigue hits, ignore remaining hits.

## ART AMMUNITION DEPLETION CHECK (11.8.0)

Roll 1d10 per mission. Modify DR (all cumulative):  
**+1:** Per volley after first volley. Intensive fire counts.  
**+1:** For any volley if all units are NW.  
**+2:** If Art unit's GenS is extended.  
Modified DR > ADV, deplete 1 unit for each Pt > ADV.  
Deplete 1 Art per volley gained by intensive fire.  
See 11.8.1 for On Hand Supply & AD.  
See 15.4.2c for unsupported AD.
