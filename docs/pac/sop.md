# SEQUENCE OF PLAY
Source: GOSS Player Aids v3 2020, Pages 3–4 (PAC 6, v4)

## JOINT PLAYER PRE-GAME DAY ACTIVITIES (3.3.1)

Both sides conduct the below phases prior to the AM Allied Player Turn. The Joint Weather Determination (3.3.1b) & the Joint Fuel Determination Phase (3.3.1e) are performed before every GT.

### Joint Air Allocation Phase (3.3.1a) (AM GT ONLY)

1) Determine APs available for the GD (20.1.0).  
2) Allocate available AP to specific missions (20.1.1).  
3) Both sides may return withdrawn Flak units to the map (20.7.2).  
4) Both sides may withdraw Flak units (20.7.2).

### Joint Weather Phase (3.3.1b)

**Conduct each GT prior to start of Allied player turn.**  
**Weather Segment:** Determine the atmospheric condition, GC, & precipitation for the GT (19.0).  
**Ground Interdiction Value Segment:** Ground interdiction values are determined (20.3.1).

### Joint Command Phase (3.3.1c) (AM GT ONLY)

**Rest GT Designation Segment:** Designate rest GTs (3.5.0).  
**Command Segment:** Both sides may:  
1) Attach and/or detach units/formations (9.6.0).  
2) Change corps and army boundaries (9.2.0).  
3) Enter/release formations to/from refit (22.4.0).  
4) Enter/release formations to/from army reserve (25.0).  

**Naval Unit Assignment Segment:** Determine the number of available naval units (11.9.0).  
**Lull Determination Segment:** Declare lulls (24.1.0).  
**Ldr Activation Segment:** Activate Ldrs (23.1.0).  
**Surrender Segment:** Determine unit surrender (15.7.1).

### Joint Logistics Phase (3.3.1d) (AM GT ONLY)

**Truck Point Assignment Segment:**  
1) Determine total number of TP for each army (16.1.0).  
2) Allocate TP to 1 of 3 tasks (16.1.2): Ammo, Fuel, Motorization.  

**Supply Interdiction Segment:** Conduct SI (20.4.0).  
**Ammo Delivery Segment:** Determine AmPs delivered & the ADV for each army & corps (16.2.0 & 16.3.0).  
**Fuel Delivery Segment:**  
1) Determine number of FPs delivered to each army (16.4.0).  
2) Allocate FP to HQ's as desired (16.4.2).  

**Depot Placement Segment:** Move or place depots (15.8.0).  
**Replacement Point Segment:**  
1) Convert Inf/Arm recycle steps to Inf/Arm ReP (22.2.1).  
2) Determine of RePs for the GD (22.3.0).

### Joint Fuel Value Determination Phase (3.3.1e)

**Conduct each GT prior to start of Allied player turn.**  
Both sides determine the fuel value for all HQ with low/no fuel (16.4.5).

## ALLIED PLAYER TURN (3.3.2)

Allied side is the active player.  
Axis side is the inactive player.

### Mode Determination Phase (3.3.2a)

Determine unit modes for this GT (5.0).  
All units must be in 1 of 5 modes:  
**Tactical** (5.1.0).  
**Maneuver Reserve** (5.4.0)  
**Prepared Assault** (5.2.0).  
**Strategic** (5.5.)  
**Exploit** (5.3.0)  
Place Art units in-battery or out-of-battery (5.6.0).

### Construction Phase (3.3.2b)

**Construction Segment:** (17.3.0).  
**Step 1:** Complete FW & bridge construction.  
**Step 2:** Advance construction of bridges.  
**Step 3:** Start construction of FWs & bridges.  

**Demolition Segment:** Eng units may conduct prepared bridge demolition (17.3.2c).

### Movement Phase (3.3.2c)

**Combat Reserve Designation Segment:** Place CR markers on eligible units, as desired (5.8.0).  
**Movement Segment:** The active player conducts steps 1 through 5 in the order given below:  
**Step 1:** Place reinforcements (21.0) & conduct required withdrawals (21.4.0). Player may conduct emergency command changes (9.4.1). The active player conducts enemy GI attacks during Step 2 through 4 against his own moving units (20.3.0).  
**Step 2:** Units in Strat mode may move (5.5.0).  
**Step 3:** The active player declares & conducts CdM attacks (7.14.0).  
**Step 4:** The active player moves units in Tac mode (5.1.0).  

1) Mech class units using Mech road movement (7.10.0).  
2) All units using non-road movement (7.11.0).  
**During step 4, players may:**  
a) Breakdown (18.4.0) and recombine (18.7.0) units.  
b) Mount and dismount units (7.12.0).  
c) Place delay markers (7.13.0).  

**Step 5:** Units in PA may move (5.2.0).  

**Command Status Segment:** Determine command status of units & HQs (9.0).  
**Quick Construction Segment:** Complete FW construction (17.3.0).

### Inactive Player Exploit Phase (3.3.2d)

**Step 1:** Formations in MR mode may enter Exploit mode (5.3.0).  
**Step 2:** The inactive player declares CdM attacks (7.14.0).  
**Step 3:** Units in Exploit may move (5.3.4) and conduct overruns (5.3.4b).  
Units moving during the Exploit Phase may be subject to ground support (GS) attacks (5.3.4c) by the active player while moving and may be subject to Art FS missions when conducting an overrun (5.2.4).  

**AM or PM GT:** May move up to ½ of their MA in each friendly Exploit Phase.  
**Night GT and ENA Period:** The player must choose to move in either the Inactive Exploit Phase or Active Exploit Phase. During the selected phase units may move their full MA.  

### Combat Phase (3.3.2e)

**Tactical Assault Designation Segment:**  
1) Designate units that will make tactical assaults (10.2.0).  
2) Attempt to remove delay markers (7.13.3).  

**Replacement Segment:** Eligible units may receive RePs (22.5.0).  
**Fire Support (FS) Segment:** Perform FS missions in order.  
**Offensive Air Support:** Active player conducts ground support (GS) missions (20.2.0).  
**Defensive Support:** Inactive player conducts FS missions; Art first (11.0), then either GS (20.2.0) and/or NGS (11.0).  
**Offensive Support:** Active player conducts FS missions; Art first, followed by NGS (11.0)  

**Attacker Status Adjustment Segment:**  
1) Remove attack markers (TA & PA) from units not adjacent to enemy units.  
2) Remove excess attack designations (TA and/or PA) not in compliance with ADV limits (12.1.0).  
3) Voluntarily attempt to remove attack designations from units adjacent to enemy units (12.2.0).  

**Ground Assault (GA) Segment:** Active player conducts GAs against enemy units (13.0). Check for:  
a) Hasty bridge demolition (17.3.2b).  
b) Bridge collapse (17.3.2a).  
c) Surrender of isolated defending units (15.7.1).  
**AS Marker Removal Segment:** Remove all AS markers (13.13.0).

### Active Player Exploit Phase (3.3.2f)

The active player conducts this phase using procedures in 3.3.2d.

### Administrative Phase (3.3.2g)

**Air Resupply Segment:** (AM & PM GTs only): Active player conducts air supply missions (20.5.0).  
**Supply Determination Segment:** Active player checks the GenS status of all HQs and then all units. Mark HQ/units OhS, OoS, and isolated as required (15.0).  
**Ammo Replenishment Segment:**  
**Step 1:** Active side expends AmP to replenish AD Art (16.3.7a).  
**Step 2:** The active side rolls 1d10 to replenish AD Art (16.3.7b).  
**Step 3:** Inactive side expends AmP to replenish AD Art (16.3.7a).  

**Fatigue Recovery Segment:** Active player may remove 1 fatigue level from all eligible units (14.3.0).  
**Delay Marker Removal Segment:** The owning player may remove any delay markers. The non-owning player may remove enemy delay markers if friendly units occupy the hex (7.13.3).

## THE AXIS PLAYER TURN (3.3.3)

The Axis turn is identical to the Allied turn, except the Axis player is the active player & the Allied player is the inactive player.

---

## UPDATE GAME TURN INDICATOR (3.3.4)

If the last turn is complete or if one player meets a sudden death victory condition, the game is over. If none of the above apply, advance the GT marker to the next GT & start a new GT.

---

## ENA FIRST PLAYER TURN (3.4.5a)

1) 1st Player Movement Phase.  
2) 2nd Player Exploit Phase (full MA). 2nd player can choose to use this phase or the 2nd Player Exploit Phase in the 2nd Player Turn.  
3) 1st Player Combat Phase.  
4) 1st Player Exploit Phase (full MA). 1st player can choose to use this phase or the 1st Player Exploit Phase in the 2nd Player Turn.

## ENA SECOND PLAYER TURN (3.4.5b)

1) 2nd Player Movement Phase.  
2) 1st Player Exploit Phase (full MA). If 1st player conducted an Exploit Phase in the 1st Player Turn, skip this phase.  
3) 2nd Player Combat Phase.  
4) 2nd Player Exploit Phase (full MA). If 2nd player conducted an Exploit Phase in the 1st Player Turn, skip this phase.

## ENA MUTUAL FATIGUE PHASE (3.4.5c)

Place all fatigue hits due to any type of ENA activity.

## REST GAME TURNS (3.5.0)

Each side must conduct 1 rest GT each GD. Rest GTs do not impact the ability of a side to conduct ENA.

### Allied Rest Turns (3.5.1)

All western Allied forces must take a rest turn each night GT unless scenario rules state otherwise.

### German Rest Turns (3.5.2)

Each GD during the Command Phase, the Axis side must secretly designate a rest GT (AM, PM, or Night).

### Rest GT Restrictions (3.5.3)

**Unit Modes:** Units may not enter PA or Exploit mode. Units in Exploit mode may remain in Exploit mode.  
**Movement:** Units may not move directly from a hex Adj to an enemy unit to another hex Adj to that same or different enemy unit.

**Exception:** Units marked with an isolated and/or OoS marker, and who move towards their source of GenS are exempt from the above Adj enemy unit restriction, although normal MHs and MP costs still apply.

### Rest Turns & Fatigue (3.5.4)

A unit incurs 1 fatigue hit for performing each of the following actions during their rest GT:  
a) Moves more than ½ of its modified MA. Apply the fatigue hit the moment a unit completes its movement.  
b) If marked with a TA marker. Apply the fatigue hit when removing the TA marker. Apply fatigue even if the unit did not conduct a TA.  
c) Defends against a GA using more than ½ its CS.  
d) Conducts any type of FS mission using more than ½ of its BF (11.4.1).  
e) If marked CR (5.8.0) and supports an Off or Def GA.

**Exception:** Units that are in a MR bonus period (5.4.4d).

### Exempt Units (3.5.4a)

The following units are exempt from rest turn movement restrictions/fatigue penalties.  
a) Mech units (including Leg units using TPs) that use Mech road movement & remain at least 6 hexes from an enemy unit (five intervening hexes).  
b) Units in a MR Bonus period (5.4.4).  
c) MP expended by units when placing delay markers does not count towards fatigue (7.13.0).  
d) Art units using in-battery movement (5.6.1).
