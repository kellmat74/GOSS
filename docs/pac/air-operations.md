# AIR OPERATION PROCEDURES
Source: GOSS Player Aids v3 2020, Pages 5–6 (PAC 7, v6)

---

## JOINT AIR ALLOCATION (AM GT)

**Air Point Availability (21.1.0):** Consult the GT Record Track to determine the number of AP available for that GD.  
**Allocation of Air Points (20.1.1):** Players must allocate available AP to specific mission types (20.1.3).  
**Air Points & Mission Types (20.1.3):**  
**Ground Support**  
**Supply Interdiction** (SI) (See Logistic Procedures)  
**Ground Interdiction** (GI)  
Air transport points (ATP) are a separate pool of Pts (20.5.0).

## FLAK UNIT WITHDRAWAL & RETURN (20.7.2)

Remove Flak capable units from the map & then return withdrawn Flak units to the map.  
- Withdrawn Flak units can support any army (i.e., not just the army assigned to when withdrawn).  
- Count non-Flak type units as outlined in 20.7.1.  
a) All HQ units.  
b) Allied Art Bn.'s (beginning in 1943).  
c) Flak type units (of any size).  
d) German & US Arm Inf Bn.'s, Arm Recon Bn.'s, Tk Bn.'s & hybrid units.  
e) CW Arm Inf & Arm Recon Bn.'s (beginning in 1943).  
- Each step of Flak type units counts as 1 point of Flak.  
- Return withdrawn Flak units by placing them on the GTRT in the next GD. On that GD place them in or adjacent to any friendly army HQ during the friendly Movement Phase. They may not move that GT.

## AP ADJUSTMENT DUE TO WEATHER (19.3.0)

Adjust the number of AP assigned to each mission (19.3.0).  
**Clear:** No Effect.  
**Partial Overcast (POvr):** Divide AP by 2 (round down).  
**Overcast (Ovr):** Divide AP by 4 (round down).  
**Storm or Snow:** Air operations not allowed.

## ANTI-AIRCRAFT (FLAK) (20.7.0)

Consult the specific mission rules for mission procedures.  
Units & city hexes may have Flak. A hex cannot contribute more than 1 Flak Pt.  
- Each German city hex has 1 Flak Pt (range of 1). The city must have been German controlled since the beginning of the scenario.  
- Scenario rules may modify terrain features & DWs that may possess Flak.  
- Each hex containing 1 of the following units has 1 Flak Pt (range of 1).  
a) All HQ units.  
b) Allied Art Bn.'s (beginning in 1943).  
c) Flak type units (of any size).  
- A hex with one of the below has 0 Flak Pt (range of 0).  
a) German & US Arm Inf Bn.'s, Arm Recon Bn.'s, Tk Bn.'s & hybrid units.  
b) CW Arm Inf & Arm Recon Bn.'s (beginning in 1943).

## AIR SUPERIORITY (ASUP) (20.6.0)

Do not assign ASup AP as escorts to GI missions.  
a) Escorts for GS, SI or air supply missions; or,  
b) Interceptors for GS, SI, GI and air supply missions.  
- APs assigned to escort a mission are in addition to the maximum number of APs allowed on each mission.

**Conducting ASup Missions:** When declaring a GS, air supply, or SI mission, declare number of mission AP & number of escort AP. The opposing player may then assign APs to intercept.  
- Each escort AP aborts 1 intercept AP, and vice versa.  
- Each surviving intercept AP aborts 1 mission AP or ATP.

## GROUND SUPPORT (20.2.0)

Enemy units and/or population features may have GS missions flown against them during a friendly/enemy Combat Phase or during an enemy Exploitation Phase (5.3.4c). Adjust the number of AP assigned based on atmospheric conditions.

### Conducting GS Missions (20.2.1):

1) Declare the target hex.  
2) Identify the observing unit (11.2.2) or if the target is air observed (8.3.0).  
3) State the number of missions & the number of AP in each mission that will be attacking the target hex.  
4) State the number of escort AP for mission. See 11.2.4 for the number of missions allowed & 11.4.3 for the number of AP allowed per mission.  
5) The opposing player declares the number of intercept ASup will fly against each mission.  
6) Resolve air superiority (20.6.0).  
7) Resolve GS mission errors (20.2.3).  
8) Resolve the GS mission (11.0).

### GS Mission Errors (20.2.3):

The GS mission player rolls 1d10. Apply all the below applicable DRMs.  
**+1:** Target hex terrain is; woods, hedgerow, Rough-2 or town.  
**+2:** Target hex terrain is; forest, bocage, or city.  
**+1:** Atmospheric condition is POvr.  
**+2:** Atmospheric condition is Ovr.  
**+1:** Per enemy Flak capable unit that is in range of the target hex (20.7.1). Apply a maximum of +2 DRM.  
**-1:** Target is in Strat mode.  
**-1:** US or CW mission in September 1944 or later.

### Results:

**9 or Greater: Abort** the GS mission, do not resolve the mission. The participating AP are used & may not conduct any other missions the same GD.  
**8: Scatter,** Conduct the GS mission, however, it does not hit the intended target hex. Roll 1d10 to determine a new GS mission target hex. If the result is:  
**0:** The hex immediately north of the intended target hex is now the target hex.  
**1 to 5:** Succeeding numbers move one hex clockwise around the original target hex with the result now being the new target hex.  
**6 to 9:** Reroll until obtaining a result of 0–5.

a) If at least 1 Pt of Flak is in the new target hex or if there are no ground units in the new target hex, treat the result as an abort.  
b) If occupied by ground units & there is not 1 Pt of Flak, resolve the mission against the new target hex no matter which side occupies the hex.  
**7 or Less: Resolve** the GS mission against the intended target hex.

## GROUND INTERDICTION VALUE DETERMINATION (20.3.0)

Conduct during the Joint Weather Phase in the Ground Interdiction Value Segment (3.3.1b). Scenario rules may list limits to the number of AP players may allocate to each sector and/or the number of total AP each side can allocate to GI.  
**Interdiction Value (20.3.1):** Determine the interdiction value for each air sector on the map. AP allocated to GI during the AM GT are in effect for both the AM & PM GTs.  
- The Allied then Axis side conducts the following for each air sector.  
1) Determine the number of AP assigned GI.  
2) Adjust the number of AP assigned based on atmospheric conditions.  
3) Determine & resolve enemy ASup missions. The GI mission player cannot assign ASup AP to escort.  
4) Adjust the number of GI mission AP based on any ASup results.  
5) See the below table & locate the adjusted AP in the top row. If the modified number of AP falls between the numbers on 2 of the columns, select the lower column.  
6) Roll 1d10 & apply the applicable weather DRM based on the atmospheric condition for the current GT:  
-1: POvr.  
-2: POvr with rain.  
-2: Ovr.    
-3: Ovr with rain.

## AIR SUPPLY (20.5.0)

Conduct air supply during the Air Resupply Segment of the Administrative Phase (3.3.2g).  
- Air supply uses air transport points (ATPs). Scenario rules will list the number of ATP available.  
- Players may only conduct air supply during AM or PM GTs.  
- The atmospheric condition must be clear or POvr.  
1) The active player declares the number of ATP conducting the mission. Do not adjust for atmospheric conditions.  
2) The active player declares the number of ASup AP escorting the mission. Adjust for atmospheric conditions.  
3) The inactive player declares the number of AP that will intercept the mission. Adjust for atmospheric conditions.  
4) Players conduct ASup (if any).  
5) The active player declares the location of the drop zone hex (DZ).  
6) The active player resolves the drop.

**The Drop Zone Hex (20.5.2):** The DZ hex must:  
- Be clear, hedgerow or rough terrain. It may contain a location or village.  
- Not be enemy occupied, or adjacent to an enemy unit.

**Resolving the Drop (20.5.3):** The mission player rolls 1d10 for each ATP. Apply the following modifiers:  
**+1:** For every 4 enemy units within 3 hexes of the DZ (round up). Each unit that is Flak capable (20.7.1) counts as 2 units if the unit is within its Flak range.  
**+1:** If the atmospheric condition is POvr.  
- If the modified DR is 8 or less, the mission delivers 1 air supply point. An unmodified DR of 0 always succeeds.

## SUPPLY INTERDICTION SEGMENT (20.4.0)

See scenario rules for the maximum number of SI missions allowed. SI missions are limited to clear & POvr only. Minimum of 2 AP & max 3 AP to each SI mission. Atmospheric conditions apply (19.3.0).  
- Players may assign AP assigned to ASup as escorts to each SI mission.  
- First, the Allied player, then the Axis player conduct all SI missions. Player conducting an SI mission is the mission player.  
1) Mission player secretly determines the number of SI missions & the number of mission & escort AP.  
2) Mission player declares the number of missions & the missions that are attacking each enemy army.  
3) Non-mission player declares the number of intercepting AP for each SI mission.  
4) The mission player selects 1 SI mission & declares number of mission AP & number of escort APs.  
5) Conduct ASup (20.6.2).  
6) Conduct Flak suppression. Each surviving intercepting AP reduces the number of Flak points by 1 (20.7.2). The following units count as 1 Pt of Flak.  
**HQ Bn.**  
**Allied Art Bn. (1943 & after)**  
**German & US Arm Inf Bn., Arm Recon Bn., & Tk Bn.,**  
**CW Arm Inf and Arm Recon (1943 & after)**  
**Each step of a Flak unit.**  
7) Mission player rolls 1d10 to determine the target.  
0–4: TP transporting fuel.  
5–9: TP transporting ammunition.  
8) Mission player rolls 1d10 to resolve mission.  
a) Modify the DR by the number of surviving mission AP:  
-1: Three surviving mission AP.  
+1: One surviving mission AP.  
+1: For every 4 surviving Flak points (round down) (20.7.2).  
b) If the modified DR is 2 or less, the mission is a success. Any other result is a failure. A successful mission reduces the number of TP assigned by 1.
