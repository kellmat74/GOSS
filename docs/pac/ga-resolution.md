# GROUND ASSAULT RESOLUTION
Source: GOSS Player Aids v3 2020, Pages 19–20 (PAC 14, v3)

---

## MANDATORY PRC (13.10.1)

Conduct a PRC (4.4.1) using the lead PR unit (13.8.1). Modify PRC using 17.4.1a and 23.2.1e. **Exception:** Defending units in Forts and Forts automatically pass mandatory PRCs. If the lead PR unit fails the PRC, add one discretionary hit to that side's total GA result.

## REDUCTION/CONVERSION OF MANDATORY HITS (13.10.2)

**Check for Mandatory Hit Reduction:** The defender may reduce the number of mandatory hits by 1 if the defending units are in a hex with an ET-3 by removing the ET-3.  
**Check for Mandatory Hit Conversion:** If all friendly units in the GA are Recon and/or CDO units (6.2.0 special stacking applies), the player may choose to convert 1 mandatory hit into 1 discretionary hit. See 13.10.2 for Exceptions. If the units do not have a valid retreat path of at least 1 hex, the player may not use the above 2 methods.

## RESOLVE MANDATORY HITS (13.10.3)

**Important:** If the GAV ratio (prior to column shifts) was less than the minimum ratio on the GAT (left most column) the attacker ignores max step loss (4.2.1a). Apply all remaining mandatory hits as step losses.  
- Max step loss applies. Eliminate 1 Z-step unit for each step loss taken (4.1.3 for other Z-step results. Assign step losses in the following order:  
**1)** Lead-P unit (13.8.1).  
**2)** Lead Arm/AT unit, if the player used Arm/AT factors (13.8.4).  
**3)** Eng unit, if an Eng unit provided a column shifts to the combat (including 13.7.4b).  
- If mandatory step losses remain after reaching max step loss, convert all remaining mandatory hits to discretionary hits.  
- If all defending units are in a Fort, and those units have met max step loss and mandatory hits remain:  
**1)** Ignore all discretionary hits.  
**2)** Inflict remaining mandatory hits to the Fort. Max step loss does not apply.  
**3)** If the Fort is eliminated and mandatory hits remain, ignore all remaining hits (mandatory and discretionary).

## RESOLVE DISCRETIONARY HITS (13.10.4)

Forts and defending units in a Fort ignore all discretionary hits. Prior to resolving discretionary hits, the player must declare if units will:  
**1)** Attempt to hold their current position (13.10.4a); **or,**  
**2)** Attempt to conduct a limited retreat (13.10.4b); **or,**  
**3)** Conduct a full retreat (13.10.4c).  
- Discretionary hits are reduced by 1 each time the player:  
**1)** Retreats all units 1 hex; **or,**  
**2)** Assigns 1 fatigue hit to all units; **or,**  
**3)** Inflicts 1 step loss to 1 unit. Max step loss may or may not apply. When resolving discretionary hits, do not eliminate 1 Z-step unit for every step loss. If all other units are eliminated & hits remain, eliminate all Z-step units.

## HOLD THE POSITION (13.10.4a)

The player must conduct a PRC using the unit with the lowest PR. The attacker uses the offensive PR, the defender the defensive PR.  
**Exception:** Units in an ET-2, ET-3, Fort or Ft Area, automatically pass this PRC. **Important:** Ignore Co. units attached to Bn.'s using 6.2.0 when determining lowest PR.  
**PRC Passed:** The units remain in their hex(es). Resolve all discretionary hits in any combination of step losses and fatigue hits (max of 2). Max step loss does not apply.  
**PRC Failed:** All friendly units must retreat. If there is only 1 discretionary hit to resolve, attack designated units may withdraw into a DW. This satisfies the 1 discretionary hit.  
- Units must retreat 1 hex (up to the unit's max retreat) for each discretionary hit.  
- Units must retreat the number of hexes equal to the remaining discretionary hits. Units may not choose a path that results in fewer hexes if a longer path is available.  
- If the units cannot retreat a minimum of 1 hex for any reason or if all units can retreat at least 1 hex, but are unable to retreat the required number of hexes, the units must absorb remaining hits as follows:  
**1)** Inflict step losses on all units, (max step loss applies) (previous step losses count).  
**2)** If the units meet max step loss and there are remaining hits, apply hits as fatigue hits on all units (max of 2).  
**3)** If after applying 2 fatigue hits there are remaining hits, take all hits as step losses (max step loss does not apply).  
**4)** If part of a retreating force retreats enough hexes to fulfill all discretionary hits, and another part cannot, the units that were unable to fulfill the total discretionary hits as retreats apply all remaining hits using 2) and 3) above.

When applying 4), if forced to eliminate all units that failed to retreat the full distance, ignore all remaining hits.

## CONDUCT LIMITED RETREAT (13.10.4b)

A player may only choose a limited retreat if all units have a valid retreat path of at least 1 hex or if all attack designated units can withdraw into a DW. Make a PRC using the same procedure in 13.10.4a.  
**PRC Passed:**  
- All defending units must retreat a minimum of 1 hex.  
- All attacking units must retreat a minimum of 1 hex or withdraw into a DW.  
- If after retreating, there are hits remaining, Resolve all hits in any combination of step losses and fatigue hits (max of 2). Max step loss does not apply.  

**PRC Failed:** Players follow the PRC failed procedures in 13.10.4a.

## CONDUCT FULL RETREAT (13.10.4c)

All units must retreat to the max distance. Units cannot stop retreating prior to fulfilling all hits unless no other options exist.  
- If after resolving all mandatory hits there is only 1 discretionary hit to resolve, attack designated units may withdraw into a DW.  
- Units must retreat 1 hex (up to the unit's max retreat) for each discretionary hit.  
- If the units cannot retreat at least 1 hex. Resolve discretionary hits in the following order:  
**1)** Inflict step losses on all units, (max step loss applies) (previous losses count).  
**2)** If max step loss is met and there are remaining hits, convert hits into fatigue hits on all units (max of two).  
**3)** If after applying 2 fatigue hits, there are remaining hits, take all hits as step losses (max step loss does not apply).  
- If all units can retreat at least 1 hex, but are all unable to retreat the number of hexes needed to fulfill all hits, absorb remaining hits in the following order:  
**1)** Inflict step losses on all units, (max step loss applies) (previous losses count).  
**2)** If the units meet max step loss and there are remaining hits, convert hits into fatigue hits on all units (max of two).  
**3)** After applying 2 fatigue hits, ignore all remaining hits.  
**4)** If part of a retreating force retreats enough hexes to fulfill all hits and another part cannot, the units that were unable to fulfill the total hits as retreats apply all remaining hits using steps 1 through 3. If forced to eliminate all units that failed to retreat the full distance, ignore all remaining hits.

## GA RETREAT PROCEDURES (13.11.0)

Stacks must retreat as a stack. Each hex of retreat must be at least 1 hex farther away from the original occupied hex. A player may not voluntarily drop off units. Units may not retreat:  
a) Into enemy occupied hexes.  
b) Into terrain or across hexside terrain prohibited to the unit's movement class or type. **Important:** If a unit could only move into a hex using Mech road movement, then it can only retreat into that hex if a road connects the 2 hexes.  
c) Mech units may not cross an unbridged river, major/great river unless using a ford.  
d) Leg units may not cross unbridged major/great rivers unless using a ford.  
e) Units may not retreat into the same hex more than once during a retreat.  
- Leg class units may retreat across an unbridged river, however, unless using a bridge or ford, the unit must stop retreat in the first hex after crossing the river hexside.  
- If there is more than 1 hex available to retreat into, choose the hex using the below criteria in the order given:  
**1)** Into a hex not adjacent to an enemy units.  
**2)** Into a hex closer (in hexes) to the unit's superior HQ.  
**3)** Into a hex containing CT.  
**4)** Along any type of road.  
**5)** Not into an overstack.  
- For each criteria:  
a) If more than 1 hex meets the criteria, move to next criteria.  
b) If only 1 hex meets criteria, unit must retreat into that hex.  
c) If no hexes meet a criteria, move down the list until only 1 hex meets a criteria. Retreat into hex meeting that criteria.  
d) If, after checking all criteria, there is more than 1 hex that meets all criteria, the player may select the retreat hex from any of those which meet all 5 criteria.

## MAX RETREAT DISTANCE (13.11.1)

Units that retreat due to a GA or overrun retreat a max of:  
a) 2 hexes if a defending Leg class unit.  
b) 4 hexes if a defending Mech class unit.  
c) 1 hex if an attacking Leg class unit.  
d) 2 hexes if attacking Mech class unit. See 16.4.5 & 7.12.3d.  
See 13.11.1 for Mech units in low/no fuel. Mech units must stop retreat in the 1st hex entered containing any of the terrain in 13.12.5 unless following a road.

## RETREAT & OTHER UNITS (13.11.2)

Units may retreat into a friendly occupied hex in violation of the stacking limits.

## RETREATING ADJACENT TO ENEMY UNITS (13.11.3)

A stack that enters a non-MCT hex Adj to an enemy unit must take 1 step loss. See 13.11.3 for exception. Z-step units cannot fulfill the step loss requirement unless they are the only unit(s). Ignore max step loss.  
- The presence of other units in the hex provides no protection from this penalty.  
- The additional step loss does not count towards fulfilling results from the GA.  

**Artillery Units:** See 13.11.4 for artillery unit retreat.

## ADVANCE AFTER GA (13.12.0)

Advancing units are subject to all MHs in the defending hex & all hexes of advance. Bridge collapse applies (17.3.2a).

## ADVANCE PROCEDURE (13.12.2)

Units are advanced individually in any order desired.  
- The path of advance consists of all hexes vacated by retreating units.  
- The first hex of advance must be into the defender's hex.  
- If all defending units are eliminated prior to retreating, the attacking player may determine the path of advance (the first hex entered must be the defending hex).  
- Advancing units must meet stacking limits (6.0).

## ADVANCE DISTANCE (13.12.3)

Units may stop at any point. Count the defender's hex as the 1st hex of advance. The max number of hexes a unit can advance is:  
**Units in Tac Mode:** 1 hex (i.e., into the defender's hex).  
**Units in Exploit Mode:** 1 hex (i.e., into the defender's hex).  
**Leg Units in PA Mode:** 2 hexes.  
**Mech Units in PA Mode:** 4 hexes.  
- If the result eliminated all defending units prior to any retreat, advancing units are limited to an advance of no more than the total unresolved hits (mandatory & discretionary)  
- Mech Recon may advance 1 hex farther than the path of retreat allows if the entire advance is solely along roads or clear terrain. When using clear terrain (not road) the Mech Recon unit cannot cross a stream and the hex must not contain any terrain other than VPs, locations & villages.  
- MHs (7.7.0) do not restrict an attacking unit advancing from its original hex into the first hex of an advance (the original defending hex). Once in the defender's hex, all MHs apply.

## ADVANCE PATH (13.12.4)

Advancing units normally follow the path of retreat chosen by the defending player. The following are exceptions to that rule:  
- Mech units may deviate from the path of retreat if all hexes entered (including the first) are clear terrain or connected by any type of road.  
- If the GA result eliminated all defending units after retreating at least one hex, the advancing units must follow the path of retreat to the hex where the elimination occurred and then, if at that point there are unfulfilled discretionary hits, the advancing player is free to determine the path of advance.

## ADVANCE RESTRICTIONS (13.12.5)

An advancing unit can never enter or cross prohibited terrain unless the advance is along a road and that road would allow such a unit to move into the hex. Unless entering a hex along a road, Mech units must stop their advance in the first hex entered that contains any of the following terrain:  
**Woods** — **Soft Ground** — **Hedgerow**  
**Forest** — **Swamp** — **RR Embankment**  
**Marsh** — **Bocage** — **Constricted Terrain**  
- Leg units that advance across a river must stop in the 1st hex after crossing the river unless using intact bridge. Ldr benefits do not nullify the above terrain restrictions.

## ETS/FORT/FT AREAS (13.12.5a)

Units must stop if they advance into a hex adjacent to an ET, Fort, or enemy intact Ft Area hex (7.9.0). There is no requirement for an enemy unit to be present in Forts & Ft Areas. Ldr benefits do not negate this requirement.

## MUD (13.12.5b)

Units may never advance more than 1 hex during mud conditions. Ldr benefits can increase an advance in mud by no more than 1 hex (max advance of 2).

## NIGHT GTS & ENA PERIODS (13.12.5c)

Units may never advance more than 1 hex during a night GT or an ENA period. Ldr benefits can increase an advance during a night GT or ENA period by no more than 1 hex (max of 2).

## FUEL (13.12.5d)

Mech units whose formation HQ is in a no fuel state may not advance. Mech units, whose formation HQ is in a low fuel state may only advance 1 hex.

## REMOVAL OF AS MARKERS (13.13.0)

Remove all AS markers at the end of the Segment.
